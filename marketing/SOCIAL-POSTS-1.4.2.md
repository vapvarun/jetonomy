## LinkedIn - Long-Form Post

Jetonomy 1.4.2 is out. Here's what we built and why.

**Show & Tell: filling the gap between forum and feed**

The most common request we heard after launch was some version of "I want a lighter-weight space for sharing - not a discussion thread, more like a quick post." Forum spaces are designed for structured back-and-forth. Activity feeds are too ephemeral. Neither is quite right for project showcases or work-in-progress posts.

Show & Tell is our answer. A first-class space type with short-form content cards, optional titles, and an inline feed layout. Members share, vote, and comment. The best posts surface naturally. It's free in 1.4.2 and available alongside any existing Forum, Q&A, or Ideas spaces.

**Ideas spaces become a real product roadmap**

Ideas has always let members submit and vote on feedback. In 1.4.2, we added status tracking: Planned, In Progress, Shipped, Declined. Status changes send notifications. The listing view shows where each idea stands.

This is a small schema change with a large UX impact. Your Ideas space goes from "a pile of suggestions you may or may not read" to a transparent, public-facing roadmap. For product teams running customer communities, this matters.

**Q&A knowledge base visibility**

Q&A accepted answers always worked well inside threads. Now they're visible from the space listing. Members can filter by Answered / Unanswered. For teams running support forums or documentation Q&A spaces, the listing view now tells members at a glance whether their question has already been answered.

**Scale and multisite**

For communities with large post volumes: cron batching at 500 rows per run with a filter for custom sizing. For multisite networks: network activation installs on every existing and future subsite automatically.

**Pro reliability**

Webhook system fully restored - all 8 listeners updated. 13 contract bugs closed. Full accessibility pass on private messaging and pattern inputs.

We run community.wbcomdesigns.com on Jetonomy in production. The cron batching fix came directly from hitting timeout issues on our own install. The multisite support came from a customer running 40+ subsites who needed a better onboarding path. This is what dogfooding a product looks like.
