### Tweet 2 — Feature Highlight: Three Space Types (Thread, reply to Tweet 1)

Let's talk about the three space types and when to use each. A thread. 1/4

**2/4**
Forum spaces work like traditional message boards — threaded replies, newest-first or top-voted sorting, follow for notifications. Good for general discussion, announcements, and anything where every reply has equal standing.

**3/4**
Q&A spaces work like Stack Overflow inside your WordPress site. The question author marks the best reply as the accepted answer. That reply pins to the top. Everything else sorts by votes.

Excellent for support, documentation, and knowledge bases.

**4/4**
Ideas spaces are community roadmaps. Members submit ideas, vote on them, and watch status changes: Open, Planned, In Progress, Done.

If you run a product and you've wanted a feedback board — this is it, on your own domain, in your own theme.

All three space types in the free plugin: https://wbcomdesigns.com/downloads/jetonomy/

### Tweet 4 — Trust Levels (Standalone)

Jetonomy's trust level system does something most forum plugins don't: it rate-limits new users automatically.

Level 0 (new members): 3 posts/day max, no link posting allowed.
Level 1: limits relax as they contribute.
Level 2: links allowed.
Level 3: can edit freely, follow topics.
Levels 4-5: you assign manually — moderator tier.

Zero configuration. Works on install.

In a standard forum, someone asks a question. They get 8 replies. Four of them are helpful, three are outdated, one is wrong. A visitor searching for the same answer has to read all 8 to find out.

**3/3**
In a Jetonomy Q&A space, the question author marks the best reply as accepted. It pins to the top — visible instantly. That answer earns the author +15 reputation points.

Better for the person asking. Better for future visitors. Better for the people who write good answers.
